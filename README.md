# Behavioral-Digital-Twin-Decision-Science-A-Self-Evolving-Market-Intelligence-Framework-

The rapid expansion of financial technology 
(FinTech) has transformed consumer interactions 
with financial services. Despite substantial 
advancements in predictive analytics and market 
intelligence, contemporary decision-support 
systems employed by FinTech firms predominantly 
rely on retrospective analyses derived from 
historical customer behavior. Such approaches often 
fail to accommodate the evolving, context
dependent, and psychologically driven nature of 
financial decision-making. Consequently, strategic 
decisions regarding customer acquisition, product 
innovation, and market positioning may be 
constrained by static representations of consumer 
behavior. 

Objective: This preprint introduces Behavioral Digital Twin 
Decision Science (BDTDS), a novel conceptual 
framework designed to enhance market research 
and strategic decision-making within FinTech 
ecosystems. The framework proposes the 
development of continuously evolving digital 
representations of consumer segments that integrate 
behavioral economics, decision science, adaptive 
artificial intelligence, and market intelligence. 
Unlike conventional market research 
methodologies, BDTDS conceptualizes customers 
as dynamic decision agents whose preferences, trust 
perceptions, and financial motivations evolve over 
time.
